## Positioning (short form)

We help Nordic restaurant and hotel operators replace admin work with AI agents — voice, email, booking. 25–35K SEK install + 8%+ monthly. Our customers are multi-location operators who today have admin staff answering phones, confirming bookings, and replying to email.

## ICP (marketing audience)

Same as sales ICP (see `../../../head-of-sales/life/areas/notion-protocol.md` and `agents/lead-researcher/life/areas/icp-playbook.md`):

- Multi-location hospitality operator in Sweden or Norway.
- Decision-makers: owner / ops director / general manager.
- Particularly: groups that have a central office doing administrative work.

## Content pillars (seed)

1. **"AI replaces your admin team"** — concrete savings stories, ROI math, admin-task-by-task automation breakdowns.
2. **Nordic hospitality trends** — reservation patterns, no-shows, labor cost pressure, language/dialect handling.
3. **Builder transparency** — behind-the-scenes on how we build an Akira agent for a specific restaurant; product gets clearer while building in public.
4. **Prospect questions** — one post per week answering an actual question pulled from Outreach Log notes.

## Language rules (locked)

- **Posts on Jules' LinkedIn feed:** English default.
- **Comments on others' posts:** match the post's language — Swedish, English, or French only. **Never Norwegian.**
- **Outbound LinkedIn DMs:** match the prospect's profile language (Swedish or English only).
- **Newsletter:** English.

## Weekly cadence

### Post-per-day rhythm (Mon / Wed / Fri) — 3 posts, 3 distinct roles

From Cold-to-Cash: the three weekly posts each have a different job. Don't collapse them into "generic content."

| Day | Post type | Role | Signal hypothesis template |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Monday** | **Lead magnet post** | Attract volume. Drive LeadShark-equivalent capture via LinkedIn engagement. Often includes a CTA like "Comment ROI to get the calculator." | "This post is designed to draw high-volume engagement from [multi-location Nordic operators curious about automation ROI]." |
| **Wednesday** | **Personal / founder post** | Build trust. Show Jules — French founder, Nordic focus, building-in-public. Less about the product, more about the person. | "This post is designed to draw engagement from [prospects on the fence, deepening trust without pitching]." |
| **Friday** | **Technical / expertise post** | Convert hesitants. Show depth — how a Vapi agent is actually configured, what went wrong on a real deploy, the non-obvious thing about Nordic reservation patterns. | "This post is designed to draw engagement from [evaluators checking whether Akira actually knows what they're doing]." |

Each post type also seeds different engager cohorts — the Monday crowd is top-of-funnel curious; Wednesday is relationship-warm; Friday is evaluating. LinkedIn Engagement Agent's reactivation flow should reflect this (a Friday engager is closer to buying than a Monday engager).

### Weekly schedule

- **Monday** — Head of Marketing's ritual heartbeat. Reviews Growth Analyst's Friday brief, updates this file, issues directives to team. Locks 3 LinkedIn posts for the week (Mon lead-magnet / Wed personal / Fri technical) with a **signal hypothesis per post**. Conversations-→-content pass from CRM Outreach Log to seed topic ideas.
- **Tue / Wed / Thu** — Content Writer drafts each LinkedIn post with its signal hypothesis. 1 newsletter section drafted mid-week.
- **Wed** — Lead Magnet Creator demos this week's magnet progress in a subtask.
- **Daily (5×)** — LinkedIn Engagement Agent: outbound engagements on others' ICP posts **and** capture + flag engagements on Jules's own posts.
- **Fri** — Growth Analyst publishes Board Brief (both funnels).

## The signal hypothesis rule

Every LinkedIn post from Jules has a one-line hypothesis in the content calendar:

> *"This post is designed to draw engagement from [segment] — [multi-location operators reflecting on labor cost / hoteliers who've tried automation / restaurant groups with admin offices / etc.]."*

Posts without a signal hypothesis get rewritten before approval. Post-publish, the Growth Analyst scores: did the hypothesis hold? Did the engagers match the predicted segment?
