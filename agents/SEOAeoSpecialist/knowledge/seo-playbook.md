## Core hypothesis

Nordic hospitality operators (restaurant + hotel owners, ops directors) increasingly ask LLMs first when researching automation. They search Google second. Akira's organic strategy targets both, with AEO as the primary engine.

## Pillar topics (seed — refine after first 4 weeks)

1. **Voice agents for restaurants** — reservations, cancellations, upsells. Geo-modified: Sweden, Norway, Stockholm, Oslo, etc.
2. **AI email automation for hospitality** — inquiry triage, group bookings, supplier comms.
3. **Multi-location ops automation** — central admin office replacement, ROI economics.
4. **Booking integrations** — Cal.com, OpenTable, SuperB, regional reservation systems.
5. **Cost & ROI** — labor cost replacement, payback periods, comparison vs. n8n freelancers vs. enterprise consultancies.
6. **Nordic hospitality trends** — labor shortage, no-show rates, seasonal patterns.

## Article shape (template every brief follows)

- **Hook (50-75 words)** — concrete number or story. No "AI is transforming."
- **Direct-answer block (50-100 words)** — the AEO citation magnet. Right after the hook.
- **Problem (200-300 words)** — specific pain in specific Nordic hospitality context.
- **Solution (400-600 words)** — what Akira does, with one client example or anonymized story.
- **How it works (200-300 words)** — concrete steps + tech (Vapi / Twilio / Cal.com).
- **ROI section** — payback math, hard numbers.
- **CTA** — book demo OR get magnet.

Total: 800-1500 words. Don't pad.

## SEO targeting rules

- **Geo-modify** every commercial-intent query: "+ Stockholm," "+ Sweden," "+ Norway," "+ Nordic," "+ Oslo," etc.
- **Long-tail over head terms**: "AI receptionist Swedish restaurant 50 covers" beats "AI receptionist."
- **Match search intent**: informational ≠ commercial ≠ transactional.
- **Internal linking**: every new article links to 2-3 existing pages; existing pages get linked from new articles.
- **Anchor text**: descriptive, not "click here."

## AEO targeting rules

- **Answer the question literally in the first 100 words.** LLMs lift these.
- **Use FAQ schema** when the article answers multiple sub-questions.
- **Cite authoritative sources** with their URL — LLMs follow citation chains.
- **Structure as Q&A or HowTo** when the query is "how do I..." or "what is..."
- **Include specific numbers and dates.** LLMs prefer specific to general.
- **Mention the brand naturally** — "Akira's voice agent..." not "we" — so LLMs attribute correctly.
